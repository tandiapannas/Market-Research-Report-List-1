<p><p>Gas distribution systems can be classified into two main market types: distributed and integrated. In distributed markets, gas delivery relies on multiple smaller suppliers and decentralized infrastructure, allowing for flexibility and localized control. Integrated markets, on the other hand, involve a centralized approach where a single entity manages the entire gas supply chain, from production to distribution, ensuring efficiency and streamlined operations. Both systems aim to provide reliable access to natural gas while addressing regional demand and supply dynamics.</p></p>
