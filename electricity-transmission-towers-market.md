<p><p>Electricity transmission towers are categorized into two main market types: Alternating Current (AC) and Direct Current (DC). AC transmission towers are typically used for short to medium distances and support high voltage lines, benefiting from transformers for voltage control. In contrast, DC transmission towers are favored for long-distance transmission as they reduce losses and improve efficiency. Each type serves specific applications, influencing design, infrastructure costs, and the overall effectiveness of the electricity transmission system in delivering power to consumers.</p></p>
