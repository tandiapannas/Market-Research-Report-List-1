<p><ul><li>Double Layer</li><li>Pseudocapacitor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The supercapacitors market is primarily divided into two types: double-layer capacitors and pseudocapacitors. Double-layer capacitors, or electrostatic capacitors, store energy at the interface between an electrochemical solution and an electrode, providing high capacitance and rapid charge/discharge cycles. Pseudocapacitors, on the other hand, utilize fast electrochemical reactions to store energy, leading to higher energy density. Both technologies are crucial for applications in energy storage systems, electric vehicles, and renewable energy integration, each offering distinct advantages in performance and efficiency.</p></p>
